ACME INDUSTRIES, INC. — Form 10-K, Item 1A (Risk Factors)
Fiscal year ended December 31, 2026  (synthetic example)

We operate in an increasingly competitive industry. During fiscal 2026 a single Connected Devices customer accounted for 19% of consolidated net revenue (compared with no customer above 8% in fiscal 2025), and the loss or material reduction of business with this customer would have a material adverse effect on our results of operations.

Our long-term debt totaled $580 million at year-end 2026, an increase of approximately 38% over the prior year, primarily reflecting borrowings to fund the acquisition of Beacon Robotics in May 2026. Our debt covenants now require us to maintain a consolidated leverage ratio of not more than 3.5x trailing-twelve-month EBITDA (revised upward from 3.0x in conjunction with the Beacon financing) and a fixed-charge coverage ratio of not less than 1.5x. As of December 31, 2026 our consolidated leverage ratio was 3.1x and we had $90 million of unused capacity on our revolving credit facility.

Following the integration of Beacon Robotics, we now derive approximately 48% of revenue from Industrial Automation, 41% from Connected Devices, and 11% from Services. The increased weighting toward Connected Devices, combined with the customer-concentration noted above, increases our exposure to short-cycle consumer-electronics demand.

In June 2026 the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency proposed a new rule that, if adopted as currently drafted, would require capital expenditures we estimate at $45 to $60 million over fiscal 2027 and 2028 at our two largest manufacturing facilities. We are unable to predict the final form of the rule.

In September 2026 we were named as a defendant in a putative class action filed in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California alleging that certain of our Connected Devices products do not comply with state-level data-protection laws. We intend to defend the matter vigorously, but the range of potential loss is not presently estimable and could be material.

The opinion of our independent registered public accounting firm on our consolidated financial statements as of and for the fiscal year ended December 31, 2026 was unqualified, but included a critical-audit-matter paragraph addressing the valuation of goodwill arising from the Beacon Robotics acquisition.
